RGB Picker

 The RGB color picker lets you create your own colors my mixing the different intensities of the three transmitted primary colors red, green, and blue (hence the acronym RGB).
Each of the three primary colors is represented by a horizontal slider. As the position of the slider nears the leftmost of its range, the color darkens; as the position nears the rightmost of its range, the color lightens. You can position the thumb of the slider by dragging it, or, if you wish, by nudging with the arrow buttons on either end of the slider. Because each slider has 256 possible positions, you can conceivably mix 16 777 216 colors!
As you mix the colors, the composite color will actively change as you move the sliders' positions. This ensures that you always see a live display of the color.
If the color you mix just happens to match that of a named HTML color, its name will appear beneath the composite color box.

If you manage to drag the composite color over the RGB picker, the RGB picker will temporarily disappear to show the custom color picker, so that you can drop the color there. Once you are done with the drag-and-drop, the RGB picker will reappear.
